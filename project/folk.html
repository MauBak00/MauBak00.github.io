Overview

Folk music is the music traditionally made by the common people. It is an extremely broad term, encompassing music made throughout the world. Folk music is often contrasted with more technically and theoretically involved "art music," and with popular music that is commercially created and distributed. Folk music laid the foundations for many other musical styles, and it continues to be celebrated in the 21st century.

China

Chinese folk music has been dated by archeologists to be 7000 years old. The pentatonic scale serves as the basis for much traditional Chinese music. Some of its styles include Xi'an drum music, Northern wind and percussion ensembles, and sorrowful Southern ballads known as nanguan.

Sri Lanka

Sri Lankan folk music features elements of nature like wild animals and flowers. Some of its styles are Kolam music, which consists of three or four notes and accompanies mask dances, Nadagam music, which is more influenced by Southern Indian art, and Sinhala light music, which blends a variety of musical influences.
